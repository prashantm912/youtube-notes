Men's Grooming Essentials: Skin, Hair, Shaving, and Scent

A practical playbook of grooming habits — from choosing the right SPF and shaving technique to hair care, barber strategy, fresh breath, and wearing fragrance well.

At a glance

  • Match your sunscreen SPF to your skin tone, and use different moisturizers for day and night.
  • Shave against the grain with your head tilted back, then exfoliate immediately to prevent ingrown hairs.
  • Wash and condition your hair roughly every three days, at night, working conditioner from the roots down.
  • Most bad breath comes from the tongue, not from between the teeth — a tongue scraper does more than extra brushing.
  • Apply fragrance to moisturized skin on the wrists and neck, never to clothing.
  • Good skin is worth prioritizing: a University of Bristol study cited here found clear skin more attractive to women than owning a nice car.

Skin care: sunscreen, moisturizer, and blemishes

Pick your SPF by skin tone

Not everyone needs the same level of sun protection. As a simple rule of thumb:

Skin tone Recommended SPF
Very light skin Around SPF 50
Moderately tan skin SPF 30 or above
Darker skin SPF 15 or above

Use different moisturizers for day and night

Using the wrong moisturizer at the wrong time can make skin look worse. During the day, choose a moisturizer with SPF and brightening ingredients such as vitamin C. At night, switch to a thicker, heavier cream containing glycolic acid or retinol so it hydrates the skin while you sleep.

Apply products gently — never rub

Rubbing products into the face damages the skin barrier, which leads to dryness and leaves skin more susceptible to acne. Apply products with the fingertips in gentle circular motions. For highly sensitive spots such as the under-eye area, use your smallest finger to lightly tap the product in.

Never pop a pimple

At best, popping pushes bacteria deeper into the skin; at worst, it does that while leaving permanent damage such as scarring. Instead, apply a small amount of topical pimple cream and leave the blemish alone.

Dark under-eye circles: skip the eye cream

Eye cream for dark circles is described here as a waste of money. A cheaper alternative: place two spoons in the freezer for 30 minutes, then hold them under the eyes. The cold metal reduces inflammation and draws away some of the blood flow that darkens the area.

Moisturize beyond the face

Most men moisturize the face but neglect the rest of the body, leaving dry, ashy patches. Make a habit of moisturizing five areas: elbows, knees, hands, chest, and neck.

Let body wash sit for three minutes

Applying body wash and rinsing it straight off gives it no time to work. Let it sit on the skin for around three minutes so it can clean, kill bacteria, and let its nourishing ingredients absorb. After rinsing, skin should feel soft and hydrated — if it does not, the body wash is not doing its job. A good body wash should nourish and hydrate the skin, not just remove dirt.

Shaving technique and body hair

Ask your barber for a full-face straight-razor shave

Dermaplaning — shaving the entire face to remove dead skin cells and fine hair — leaves skin smoother and improves product absorption, but clinic treatments can be expensive. A cheaper route: next time your barber lines you up, ask him to shave your whole face, including the upper cheeks and forehead, with a straight razor.

Revive a dull razor on denim

If a razor is dull, old, or not cutting cleanly, stroke it a few times against a pair of jeans and it will cut like new.

Shave upward, then exfoliate

Shaving upward means shaving mostly against the grain, which gives a much cleaner result. Because going against the grain raises the risk of ingrown hairs, exfoliate the area immediately after shaving. No exfoliating product on hand? Mix a little sea salt with water and use that.

Tilt your head back when you shave

Tilting the head back pulls the skin tighter, letting the blade glide closer for a tighter shave. It also gives a better angle for lining up the beard and spotting stray long hairs.

Keep the chest hair, groom everything else

Regrown chest hair comes back prickly — uncomfortable under a shirt and unpleasant for a partner. More importantly, in a study of women's body-hair preferences cited here, only 14% preferred a fully shaved chest; the rest preferred some hair, finding it more masculine. The private area, abs, and legs are a different story: shaving those areas makes them look bigger and more defined.

Make your eyebrows symmetrical

Tidy eyebrows add balance and symmetry to the face, but only when done correctly. You need an eyebrow shaver and a comb or pen. Line the pen up vertically from the middle of the nostril: any hair growing past that line gets shaved off. Repeat on the other side.

Match your facial hair to the occasion

Studies cited here suggest facial hair changes how women perceive men in different situations:

  • Clean shaven — preferred when you hold a position of power and want to appear more trustworthy.
  • Light stubble — preferred for attracting attention on a night out.
  • Full beard — preferred by women looking for a long-term partner.

Hair care: washing, conditioning, and styling

Wash and condition every three days — and do it at night

A 2021 study cited here found that men who did not wash their hair appropriately began losing it at a younger age. Washing and conditioning roughly every three days protects against early hair loss. Wash at night rather than in the morning: it removes the bacteria accumulated through the day before it transfers to your pillowcase, where it can end up causing acne.

Condition from the roots down

Start applying conditioner at the roots — the base of the hair if it is short — and work down. Covering the entire hair shaft reduces dryness, brittleness, and scalp issues such as itchiness and dandruff, and conditioning this way can even reduce the chances of hair loss.

Product quality matters here: many cheap liquid conditioners are filled with harsh chemicals that dry hair out and contribute to hair fall. Favor products made with natural, hydrating ingredients.

Ditch the comb-over

If you are losing your hair, you have three honest options: a hair transplant, taking better care of the hair you have, or shaving it all off. Never attempt a comb-over or push the hair forward to hide hair loss — everyone can tell.

Try the buzz cut at least once

Every man should shave his head at least once, because you will not know whether it suits you until you do. If it looks bad, it humbles you and builds confidence; for most men, it looks great.

Choose the right styling product

Of the many hair products available, only a few actually make men look better. Ranked from worst to best:

  1. Gel — makes hair look too greasy.
  2. Hairspray — better, but leaves hair crunchy.
  3. Styling cream — a neutral, all-around okay product.
  4. Clays and pomades — great hold and finish.
  5. Texture powders — great hold plus a natural, texturized, just-rolled-out-of-bed look.

Stick to the bottom three on that list — styling cream, clays and pomades, and texture powders.

Protect your hair from the sun

Hair takes sun damage too. Each time you apply sunscreen to your face, dab a little of what is left into your hair to help protect it from sun exposure, graying, and hair loss.

Getting more from your barber

  • Try multiple barbers before committing. Sampling different barbers lets you try multiple styles and find what suits your hair type and desired hairstyle best.
  • Match the haircut to your face shape. If you do not know your face shape, take an online test. Then search for haircuts suited to that shape and bring the images to your barber for the perfect cut.
  • Book the first slot on Monday morning. The shop is empty and the barber is fresh from a weekend of rest, so you get more time and attention — and a better cut — than on a Friday night at the end of a long working day.
  • Maintain the back of your neck between cuts. Set your phone to the selfie camera and stand with a mirror in front of you; using both together, you can guide yourself as you trim upward to clean up the back of the neck and keep the haircut looking fresh.

Fresh breath and oral hygiene

Floss before you brush

Flossing first softens the food debris and plaque buildup between the teeth, making brushing more effective at cleaning them properly and eliminating bad breath.

Bad breath lives on the tongue

Almost all the bacteria behind bad breath sit on the tongue, not between the teeth. A tongue scraper — around two dollars online — clears the white coating off the tongue and keeps breath fresh.

Replace your toothbrush every three months

A toothbrush accumulates a lot of bacteria, which makes breath smell worse. Make it a rule to replace the brush — or the head, if you use an electric toothbrush — every three months.

Fragrance: wearing scent well

Match the scent to the event

Like clothing, fragrance should align with where you are going. For school or work, choose a light cologne with fruity notes; for a date, opt for something heavier with woody notes.

The five rules of applying fragrance

Most men waste fragrance by applying it wrong. Follow these five rules:

  1. Apply it to skin, never to clothing.
  2. Apply it to moisturized skin, right after the shower.
  3. Never spray a cloud and walk into it.
  4. Use it only on the wrists and neck.
  5. Try mixing your scents.

Make it last with petroleum jelly

Before applying fragrance, dab a little petroleum jelly on the wrists and neck. It makes the scent project more strongly and helps it last longer.

Small details: nails and ears

Cut your nails right after a shower

Whether fingernails or toenails, cut them immediately after stepping out of the shower. The nails are noticeably softer, which gives a closer, cleaner cut every time.

Stop using cotton swabs in your ears

Cotton swabs just push bacteria and ear wax deeper into the ear. Instead, fill a spray bottle with a mix of water and soap and use it to flush the wax out.
